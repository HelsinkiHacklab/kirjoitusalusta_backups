What you should check before printing
  • Check that there's nothing blocking the motors under the unit (power cables or something else)
  • Check that the endstops are working, especially the Z-endstop

Printing instructions
  • See documentation on Torsti (TODO: Move here?)
1) Turn the ATX power on
2) Open Skeinforge and use it to convert your .stl file to gcode. Click "craft" to select your model. You can see the conversion process in a terminal window.
3) Open RepSnapper to print your model from gcode. In the simple tab, choose "connect" to printer and when connected, click "Load GCode" and choose the generated file. It might take a while to load (see the processbar at the top) you need to move the viewpoint to refresh the view (zoom in and out for example)
4) Go to Interactive tab and switch the heat on (180 to 200 deg with PLA material) it always depends with the material you are using, so do try it out! cheap PLA tends to need more temp.
5) Lower the extruder so that it is just above the print surface and use the X/Y controls (+1/+10) to move the extruder to good place to start your print (depends on the center point of your loaded gcode) by default you should center the head to the center of platform
6) Click on "Zero XY" and "Zero Z" to assign the starting point of your print
7) Go to print tab and print

Tip to get a good start with printing
Current firmware doesn't extrude any plastic before it actually starts to print. This should be done to get a stable flow of extruding the plastic. For example - Ultimaker first extrudes a tiny bit of PLA before it starts to print the real model, it will make a square around the print. The square is done because when starting to extrude it usually takes a moment to get the flow and basicly you lose some bits of your base from the model if your printer isn't extruding properly, in some cases this might make the whole print to fail.

When you start to print with Torsti, you might notice that it's not actually extruding the plastic when it starts to print, you should wait that it starts to actually print the first layer. When the printing quality looks good hit the "reset printing" button at the print tab, quickly clean the platform with a knife etc. and when the printer starts doing the same thing again now it should start with a good print right away.

Printer not working?
1 ) No power?

  • Check if the power outlet is switched on.

Easy way to see if you get power is to check if stepper drivers are on. They have green/orange leds on board and are located in front of the unit. 

  • If power outlet is turned on but you still don't get power check that ATX power supply is switched on, there's a power switch behind the unit. Powersupply cooling fan in this unit might be stopped if there's no load so check the power some other way. 

  • If the printer was turned off from the power outlet while printing (when something went wrong) ATX might not turn on before you take the jumpercable off from the ATX connector and put it back (That is the short green wire forced into ATX connector to pin 14? and GND).

2) No connection to printer with Repsnapper (no connection sound) even cables and everything else seems to be ok.

  • Try reseting the printers mainboard, there's a yellow reset switch in the board.

3) Stepper is jammed? 

  • One of the steppers is not moving either direction? it might be in wrong angle. You can feel stepper buzz when pressing finger on its axel. But watch out, steppers might be burning hot!  Unpower Reprap and try to push/pull it manually to correct position. Repower and test.

Creating models (General stuff)
  • General:
    • Solid modelling tools generally produce clean and printable model, mesh modelling tools require extra steps to clean the model
  • Solid modeling tools:
    • OpenScad
      • Programmatic creation of solid shapes.  Powerful, but a bit complicated.  Works well.
      • buggy preview rendering (doesn't draw everything correctly)
        • Use render() call on strategic places or always render whole thing to GCAL (the preview can explode if the CSG tree gets too complex)
      • Lots of existing code:
        • github.com/solidcode/MCAD
        • Thingiverse.com
      • Forks
        • github.com/solidcode
    • ShapeSmith
      • Parametric solid modelling
      • WebGL UI, online or local
      • New, limited features
    • Freecad
      • supposed to be better than OpenScad?
      • not as simple as OpenScad, has more "easy to use" tools etc. (this is either con or pro)
  • Mesh modellers
    • Using Google Sketchup to create and print 3D models
      • Very intuitive user interface
      • Use the plugin http://www.guitar-list.com/download-software/convert-sketchup-skp-files-dxf-or-stl to export the 3D model to SPL format
      • Brett's rewrite of skp-to-dxf allows "Right click to export": http://brettbeauregard.com/download/skp_to_Custom.rb rm: http://brettbeauregard.com/blog/2011/06/sketchup-to-stl-plugin-with-no-dialogs/comment-page-1/#comment-19324
      • use manifold plugin for finding errors from your model http://forums.sketchucation.com/viewtopic.php?f=323&t=25466
      • there's also stl importer plugin available
      • if you want to import vector graphics you need to buy pro version or you can download the evalution version of Sketchup 7 pro and then get a free plugin to import dxf format. It's supposed to work even after you lose the pro evalution and the Sketchup converts back to free version. You can probably save the imported vectors to skp and open them with latest version of sketchup.
    • Blender
      • hard to learn but otherwise a great tool for 3D printing. 
      • Supports stl export without a any extra plugins
      • Has a great clay/freeform tool
      • there's a gcode simulator plugin for blender at thingiverse, looks good and is probably helping a lot when creating a print.
    • Alibre
    • costs some money but should be very good for modeling, it's a pro cad tool after all.

  • Creating Gcode for printing
    • http://slic3r.org/ recommended but not usable with Torsti (but it will be soon!)
    • Skeinforge
    • Cura: https://github.com/daid/Cura/wiki
